# Facebook Creative Direction — هفار

> Facebook is where the Egyptian social fabric lives. Hvar's audience discovers, discusses, shares, and decides here. This is the primary community platform — not a secondary distribution channel. Every piece of Hvar Facebook content is designed for this audience first, everything else second.

---

## Platform Role

Facebook does for Hvar what no other platform does: community. The Egyptian Facebook behavior is specific — groups, shares, comment threads that run to hundreds of entries, video views in the millions. A recipe video that gets shared into a housewives' group in Alexandria reaches an audience that paid advertising could not buy.

Facebook's specific function for Hvar:
- **Discovery:** Women who don't know Hvar yet encounter it through shared content — a recipe video a friend shared, a product post in a kitchen group
- **Trust-building:** Comments where real customers confirm the product, where the brand responds warmly and specifically, where Chef Sara's endorsement is visible — this is social proof at scale
- **Video performance:** Facebook's native video algorithm still rewards quality content, especially cooking-related videos. Longer watchtime on recipe demos pays back in organic reach.
- **Purchase decision support:** The woman who is deciding whether to buy comes to Facebook to see what others say. The comment thread on a product post is a sales mechanism.
- **Community ownership:** Hvar's Facebook page is a community home, not a broadcast channel. The distinction matters in every content and response decision.

## Content Pillars

### Pillar 1: المطبخ الحقيقي (The Real Kitchen) — Recipe and Demo Content

**What it is:** Recipe videos featuring Hvar appliances used as intended, in real Egyptian kitchens, making real Egyptian food. The food is the hero. The appliance is the enabler.

**Format:** Native Facebook video (not Reels-first, though Reels can be published simultaneously). 60–180 seconds optimal. Subtitled in Arabic, always.

**Frequency:** 3–4x per month, more during cooking seasons (Ramadan, Eid, winter)

**Visual style:**
- Warm kitchen environment, natural or warm artificial light
- Hands visible in frame for the key technique moments
- The Hvar product is used, not posed
- Food result shown at the end — the payoff visual that drives sharing

**Arabic caption formula:**
```
[The dish or technique — not the product]
[One specific detail that makes this interesting]
[Soft product tie-in or chef credit]
[One question or invitation to engage]
[CTA — product link in first comment]
```

Example:
```
الكنافة في ربع ساعة — من غير فرن.
السر في توزيع الحرارة — لما الهواء يوصل لكل ناحية، الكنافة بتطلع متساوية من كل جهة.
شيف سارة بتشاركنا الطريقة باستخدام الفريير الهوائي من هفار.
عندكم طريقة تانية للكنافة؟ شاركونا في التعليقات.
```

**What the post is trying to DO:** Build awareness and authority. Secondary conversion through product link in first comment. Primary goal is watch-time and shares.

### Pillar 2: الثقة أولاً (Trust First) — Social Proof and Guarantees

**What it is:** Customer testimonials, warranty reminders, COD explanations, delivery experience posts. Content that reduces purchase anxiety.

**Format:** Single image with testimonial or graphic. Short video (30–60 seconds) of customer unboxing or using product. Static graphic for trust mechanics.

**Frequency:** 2x per month ongoing. Heavier around promotional periods or new product launches.

**Visual style:**
- Customer testimony: real photo or screenshot with privacy handled appropriately (face visible only with permission, otherwise quoted text on brand graphic)
- Trust mechanic graphics: Hvar red/brass on ivory, clean typography, one message per graphic. No cluttered trust-seal stacking.
- The design reads premium, not bargain-brand. The trust is communicated by the confidence of the statement, not by a badge graphic that looks like it came from a cheap template.

**Arabic caption formula:**
```
[The trust statement — direct, not as a claim but as a fact]
[What it means for the buyer — specifically]
[Optional: third-party voice (customer quote)]
[CTA if applicable]
```

Example:
```
ضمان ٣ سنين — وده مش مجرد كلام.
لو في أي مشكلة في أي جزء من المنتج خلال ٣ سنين، هفار بتصلحه أو بتستبدله.
عميلتنا دعاء كتبت: "الموتور اتعطل بعد سنة ونص — جيبولي موتور جديد في أسبوع."
اشتري دلوقتي بضمان هفار ←
```

**What the post is trying to DO:** Reduce pre-purchase anxiety. Close the hesitant buyer. Primary goal is reach among audiences who have engaged with product posts but not converted.

### Pillar 3: مع الشيف (With the Chef) — Chef Endorsement Content

**What it is:** Content featuring Chef Sara Abdelsalam (and any future endorsed chefs) cooking, teaching, or recommending. This is distinct from general recipe content — the chef's presence and voice are the differentiating element.

**Format:** Video primary (60–240 seconds). Photo carousels for recipe step-by-step.

**Frequency:** 1–2x per month per endorsed chef

**Visual style:** Follows the chef's authentic visual aesthetic, not a forced brand template. The chef's kitchen or a real kitchen set. The chef's normal way of filming and presenting. Brand integration is natural: the Hvar product is in use, mentioned when the chef would naturally mention it, not announced as a sponsorship mid-video.

**How to feature the brand:**
- The product is visible in use throughout the video — not featured with a close-up brand logo shot
- The chef mentions the product as they would mention any tool they are using: "أنا بستخدم الفريير الهوائي هنا عشان —" followed by the actual reason, not "الرائع الجديد من هفار"
- A product graphic or link appears in the caption or first comment — not as an overlay on the video

**What the chef's voice says vs. what the brand voice says:**
- The chef talks about the food, the technique, the result
- The brand voice (in the caption) adds the product detail, the link, the trust signal
- The caption does not repeat what the chef said in the video — it adds the commercial layer the chef shouldn't need to carry

**Arabic caption formula for chef content:**
```
[The chef's name + the dish — respect the chef's billing]
[What makes this technique or recipe distinctive]
[Product detail: what Hvar appliance was used and why]
[Product link CTA in first comment]
```

**What the post is trying to DO:** Authority transfer. The chef's credibility becomes the brand's credibility. Primary goal is new audience reach via the chef's followers.

### Pillar 4: المنتج في الواجهة (Product in Focus) — Direct Product Posts

**What it is:** Direct product introduction or feature highlight posts. These are not catalog posts — they are product stories told with specific, useful detail.

**Format:** Carousel (4–6 slides) or single image. Video if a specific product function demonstrates better than static.

**Frequency:** 1–2x per week for launches or feature highlights; 2–4x per month in steady state

**Visual style:**
- Lead slide: product in context (not white background unless necessary for visual clarity on a specific feature)
- Feature slides: one specific benefit per slide, stated in warm authority language
- Final slide: price, COD badge, warranty line, CTA
- Consistent brand design: warm ivory or brand red where appropriate, Cairo type, no crowding

**Arabic caption formula:**
```
[The problem the product solves or the need it serves — not "introducing our new product"]
[The specific product detail that addresses that problem]
[Price + installment line]
[Trust line: COD + shipping + warranty]
[CTA]
```

Example:
```
لما الهندباية بتاخد نص ساعة — المشكلة مش في المطبخ، في الخلاط.
الخلاط الجديد من هفار موتور ١٢٠٠ واط — يعمل عجينة وسلطة وجبنة بيضا من غير ما يفقد طاقته.
من ١٢٩٩ جنيه — أو ١٠٨ جنيه شهريًا على ١٢ شهر عن طريق فاليو.
أقل من اللي تتوقع — مع شحن مجاني وضمان سنتين وادفعي لما الطلب يوصلك.
اطلبيه دلوقتي ←
```

**What the post is trying to DO:** Conversion. Primary goal is product page visits and add-to-cart.

## Video Standards

### Reels vs. Native Video vs. Live

- **Native video** is primary for recipe demos and chef content. Facebook's algorithm rewards native upload over YouTube link.
- **Reels** are used when the content is optimized for vertical (9:16) and short (15–60 seconds). Reels and native video serve different placements; produce both when the content budget allows.
- **Facebook Live** is used for launches, Q&A with Chef Sara, real-time cooking events. Lives require advance promotion (post the day before, reminder hour before). Save the replay.

### Aspect Ratios
- Feed native video: 16:9 (landscape) or 4:5 (portrait — higher real estate in feed)
- Reels: 9:16 (vertical, full screen)
- Square (1:1) is acceptable for product highlight videos; not preferred for recipe demos

### Subtitle Rules

**Arabic subtitles are mandatory on every video.** A significant portion of the Facebook audience watches without sound. A recipe video with no subtitles loses that audience entirely. Subtitles:
- Always Arabic, always on. Not auto-generated captions that are reviewed once — properly timed, correctly written subtitles.
- Large enough to read on mobile without squinting: minimum 20px equivalent text, high contrast
- Style: white text, light drop shadow or dark background bar. Hvar brand fonts if possible (Cairo), but legibility takes priority over brand consistency in this case
- Do not subtitle every word — subtitle meaningful phrases, not word-by-word

### Thumbnail Standards

Native Facebook video uses auto-generated thumbnails or a selected frame. For longer recipe content:
- Select a frame that shows the food result — not a prep step, not the appliance
- The image should make someone who sees it while scrolling stop scrolling
- Avoid text on thumbnail (Facebook's ad-text rules apply to organic video thumbnails in some contexts; cleaner anyway)

### First-3-Seconds Hook Formula

The first three seconds determine watchtime. The entire video depends on this window.

Formula for Egyptian Facebook audience:
1. **Show the result first** — the finished كنافة, the cut chicken, the smooth hummus — before the process
2. **Or use a specific surprising claim** — "الكنافة بتتعمل في ١٥ دقيقة" on screen, not spoken, while the action starts
3. **Never:** brand logo intro, presenter greeting, product feature listing, text-over-black intro card

The first frame must be the most visually compelling frame in the video.

## Trust Content

### Customer Testimonials

- Collect them actively: WhatsApp message after delivery, comment response follow-up, direct DM after positive comment
- Format: direct quote on a clean card (Cairo typeface, warm brand colors), or screenshot with customer's name and photo where permission given
- Never fabricate or embellish — if a real customer said "بيشتغل كويس، مبسوطة" that is valuable. Dress it up into "منتج رائع غيّر حياتي" and it reads fake to the next person.
- Post frequency: 2x per month minimum; heavier post-Eid or post major promotional period when recent orders are high

### COD Confirmation Posts

When COD remains the dominant payment method in Egypt, showing that it works smoothly is itself a trust signal. Post format:
- "الطلب وصل — والعميلة افتكرت لما شافته" — a short story post (not just the confirmation graphic)
- The story is: ordered, arrived on time, inspected, paid, happy. This is the actual flow. Show it.

### Warranty Reminder Posts

- Not just "we have warranty." The specific experience: what happens when you claim, how long it takes, what the outcome is.
- Pair with a real story when available. When not available, state the process specifically: "تكلميني على الواتساب — بنعمل معاها تيكت صيانة وبنقدر نوصل لها في ٧٢ ساعة."

## Ad Creative Standards

### Carousel Ads

- Slide 1: the hook — food result, or the specific benefit claim
- Slides 2–4: product in context, one benefit per slide
- Final slide: price, trust line (COD + warranty + free shipping), CTA button
- Never lead a carousel ad with the product on a white background

### Single Image Ads

- Must have Hvar red visible but not dominant (10% of the image)
- Product in use or food result — never isolated product on white
- One line of Arabic text maximum on the image itself
- Headline in Arabic: benefit-led, not feature-led

### Video Ads

- Same first-3-seconds rule applies and is more important in paid context
- Subtitles mandatory (paid video often plays without sound in feed placement)
- CTA card at the end: product name, price, "اطلبي دلوقتي" button equivalent

### CTA Copy Formula

For Egyptian audience, the most effective CTA language is specific and action-oriented:
- "اطلبيه دلوقتي — بيوصلك في ٤٨ ساعة"
- "افحصيه لما يوصل — وادفعي بعدين"
- "شوفي التفاصيل والسعر"
- NOT: "Buy Now", "Shop Now" (English), "اشتري" without context

## What to NEVER Post

- **Generic stock photography** of families, kitchens, or cooking that does not look Egyptian — the audience reads inauthenticity immediately
- **Anchor pricing theater** ("كان ٣٠٠٠ — دلوقتي ١٢٩٩!") without a real, temporary, specific discount. If the product is always sold at 1,299, the "was 3,000" is a lie. The audience knows.
- **Flash sale urgency** ("آخر ٢٤ ساعة!", countdown timers) that is not factually and specifically true — this destroys the premium brand feeling
- **Grammatically broken Arabic** — any post with hamza errors, wrong gender agreement in feminine-addressed copy, or MSA-slang mixing should not go live
- **Posting errors** — wrong price, product name mismatch, broken link in caption. Verify every post before publication. A price error on a Facebook post creates customer service nightmares and undermines trust.
- **Direct competitor attack content** — never name Sokany or Tornado in a comparison post. The brand speaks for itself. Comparison content is allowed only in a factual, specific, non-attack format.
- **Content that does not have a clear purpose** — every post answers: is this building awareness, building trust, or driving conversion? If the answer is "it seemed nice," it should not be posted.

## Comment Community Standards

### Tone in Comments

The same warm authority voice that appears in content applies in comments. A Hvar customer service response in a comment is brand copy. It matters.

**Do:**
- Respond to every direct question, usually within 4 hours during business hours
- Answer specifically — if someone asks about a product's wattage, give the wattage, not a redirect to the website
- Acknowledge criticism warmly and move it to a private channel: "نأسف على الموقف ده — ممكن تكلمينا على الواتساب عشان نحل الموضوع بسرعة؟"
- Thank positive comments with a specific response, not just a like or a generic "شكرًا"

**Don't:**
- Copy-paste the same response to multiple different comments — the audience sees this and reads it as automation
- Get defensive about criticism in a public comment thread
- Delete critical comments (unless they violate community guidelines: spam, slurs, or unrelated content) — a deleted comment becomes a screenshot story
- Leave questions unanswered for more than 24 hours

**Response time expectation:** During business hours (9am–6pm, Sat–Thu): within 4 hours. After hours: within 12 hours, with a note if the question requires action that will happen next business day.

**Escalation:** Any comment reporting product failure, delivery failure, or serious service issue should be escalated to the appropriate team within 2 hours during business hours, with a holding response in the comment within that time: "بنتواصل معاكِ دلوقتي على الواتساب عشان نحل الموضوع — آسفين على الإزعاج."
