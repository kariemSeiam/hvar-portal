# TikTok Creative Direction — هفار

> TikTok is the most demanding platform in the mix and the one with the highest potential for organic reach. The rules here are different from Facebook and Instagram. Read this file before producing any TikTok content.

---

## Platform Role

TikTok's specific job for Hvar:
- **Cultural relevance:** Being on TikTok signals that the brand is contemporary. An Egyptian homemaker in her late 20s or early 30s who is active on TikTok notices brands that appear there and notices brands that don't.
- **Organic reach:** The TikTok algorithm delivers content to non-followers based on engagement quality. A recipe video that gets 70% watchtime from people who don't follow Hvar will be distributed to more non-followers. This is organic reach at a scale that Facebook's algorithm no longer offers and Instagram's Reels only partially replicate.
- **Recipe discovery:** The #وصفات hashtag ecosystem on Egyptian TikTok is massive. Women discover new recipes, new techniques, and by extension new tools, through this ecosystem daily. Hvar belongs in this ecosystem.
- **Appliance education:** Showing what a product actually does in 30 seconds — the real operating sound, the real result, the real quantity — is the most effective product demo ever invented. A feature list in a description is a claim. A video of the result is proof.
- **Algorithm-driven exposure:** TikTok's distribution is interest-based, not follower-based. The follower count on TikTok matters far less than the content quality and the first-2-second retention rate.

**Who Hvar is reaching on TikTok:** The 25–35 segment of the core audience. Women slightly younger than the median Facebook user who are looking for practical content and discovering new tools through what their algorithm serves them. This audience is already on TikTok for cooking content — Hvar's job is to be the brand that appears there with the best version of that content.

## The TikTok Creative Mandate

**Be useful in the first 2 seconds or lose them.**

This is not a creative suggestion. It is the platform's operating constraint. TikTok's algorithm decides whether to distribute a video based heavily on the first few seconds of watchtime and completion rate. A video that loses 60% of viewers in the first 3 seconds will not be distributed. A video that retains 70% through the first 10 seconds will.

**The recipe demo IS the ad.** There is no separate "brand message" and "content" on TikTok. The brand message is the content, or there is no brand message. If a Hvar video is not something a TikTok user would organically save and share — not because it is branded, but because it is genuinely useful or satisfying — it will not perform.

**No traditional ad formats work here.** A video that opens with the Hvar logo, a tagline, and a product announcement loses the audience in the first 2 seconds. The product earns its place by being naturally in the frame, doing something impressive. The brand earns its place by producing the video — not by announcing itself at the top.

## Content Types

### 1. Recipe Demo (Core)

The primary Hvar TikTok content type. A complete recipe, or the critical technique step of a recipe, showing the Hvar appliance as the tool that makes it work.

**Creative formula:**
1. **Open on the result** (0–2 seconds): the finished dish, the cut open product, the steam — before any cooking starts. This is the hook. The viewer needs to see the destination to commit to the journey.
2. **Cut to the start of the process** (2–5 seconds): the raw ingredients, the setup. No preamble. No presenter introduction. Mid-action.
3. **The key technique moment** (5–20 seconds): the step that is most satisfying to watch and most dependent on the Hvar appliance. The pour into the blender, the air fryer closing with food inside, the dough coming together in the mixer. Show the appliance doing the work.
4. **The transformation** (20–35 seconds): before and after within the cook. Ingredients going in → result coming out. The moment of reveal.
5. **The payoff** (35–45 seconds): the finished dish. Ideally: a close-up of the texture, a cut, a taste. The viewer should finish the video wanting to make this.

**Duration:** 45–60 seconds is optimal for recipe demos. Long enough to be satisfying, short enough not to test the audience's patience.

**The product's presence:** The Hvar appliance is in frame throughout the cooking steps. It is not announced. Its label may or may not be visible — this is fine. What matters is that the viewer associates the result with the tool that produced it. A text overlay or end card can name the product once.

---

### 2. Appliance Tip (Educational)

A single useful, non-obvious tip about one of Hvar's product categories. Not a recipe. Not a product demo. A piece of genuinely useful information that earns the audience's time.

**Creative formula:**
1. **Hook** (0–2 seconds): text on screen or spoken: "هل عارفتِ إن الفريير الهوائي يعمل ده؟" or "الغلطة اللي كل الناس بتعملها في الخلاط."
2. **The tip** (2–20 seconds): show it happening. The action, not just the explanation.
3. **The result** (20–30 seconds): what is different. Why this tip matters.

**Duration:** 20–35 seconds. This content type is short by design — the specific value is dense.

**Examples:**
- "افتحي باب الفريير في منتص الوقت — ده مش خسارة حرارة، ده بتكلّل الدجاج من الجنبين"
- "الخلاط بيشتغل أحسن لو الماء أول — قبل المكونات الصلبة"
- "الكاسة بتاعة الخلاط بتتسخّن لو بتعمل كميات كبيرة — خليها تبرد دقيقتين بين الدفعات"

---

### 3. Before and After Transformation

The most immediately shareable format. Two states — before and after — joined by a transition, with the Hvar appliance as the transformation agent.

**Creative formula:**
1. **Before** (0–3 seconds): the raw, unprocessed state — the whole vegetables, the dry ingredients, the uncooked meat
2. **Transition** (3–5 seconds): a visual cut or a smooth transition (pour into appliance, close the lid, press start)
3. **After** (5–15 seconds): the result — smooth, cooked, transformed, finished

**The transition is everything.** The moment of going from "before" to "after" is what makes this format satisfying. It must be a clean, satisfying visual cut. Not a fade, not a dissolve — a cut or a reveal.

**Duration:** 15–25 seconds. Faster is often better for this format.

---

### 4. Kitchen Organization

A secondary content type that extends Hvar's territory from cooking to the kitchen as a space. Appliance storage, organization, setup — this content builds brand presence in a broader kitchen-life context.

**Creative formula:**
- Satisfying reveal of an organized space
- The Hvar appliances visible as part of the kitchen (not isolated as products)
- A practical tip: "الفريير يحتاج مسافة ٤ سنتي من الحيط"

**Frequency:** Low — 1 per month maximum. This is supplementary, not core.

---

### 5. Trending Sound + Cooking

TikTok's trending audio creates opportunities for organic reach when matched with compelling visual content. This content type rides a trending sound while showing cooking action that is inherently satisfying.

**How to use this format:**
- The trending sound must match the energy of the cooking action — fast cuts for high-energy sounds, satisfying close-ups for smooth sounds
- The brand does not need to be in the audio — it is in the visual
- This format is more experimental; use when the trending sound and the cooking content match naturally, not forced

**Risk:** trending sounds can become associated with memes or content that ages badly. Review the sound's context before using.

---

### 6. Chef Collab (Duet, Stitch)

Chef Sara (or another endorsed chef) creates content on their own TikTok account. Hvar's TikTok uses Duet or Stitch to add a layer: showing the Hvar appliance used to execute what the chef demonstrates, or adding the "what to buy" layer to the chef's recipe content.

**Duet format:**
- Chef's video on one side, Hvar showing the specific appliance step on the other
- Works best when the chef's video focuses on technique and the Hvar side shows the tool

**Stitch format:**
- Use a clip from the chef's video as the hook (3–5 seconds)
- Continue with: "and here's what she used to make it" — show the appliance in action

**Permission:** Always coordinate with the chef before stitching or duetting. Do not use a chef's content without explicit agreement on how their content is used commercially.

## Hook Formulas for Egyptian TikTok

The hook is the first 1–2 seconds. These formulas have a demonstrated pattern of working for Egyptian cooking content:

**The curiosity gap:**
- "هتعمل إيه لو عارفتِ إن الكنافة بتتعمل في ربع ساعة؟"
- "الغلطة اللي ٩٠٪ من البنات بتعملوها في الفريير الهوائي"
- "اللي حد ما قالكِ عن الخلاط الكهربائي"

**The result-first visual hook** (no text, immediate impact):
- Open on the finished dish, steaming, beautifully lit
- Open on the moment of cutting through a perfectly cooked item — the satisfying cross-section
- Open on the pour of a smooth blended product

**The relatable Egyptian kitchen problem:**
- "لما الدجاجة بتاخد ساعة ونص في الفرن..." (then show the air fryer result in 25 minutes)
- "لما العجينة بتتلف من التعجين الكتير..." (then show the mixer doing it correctly)

**The specific time/quantity surprise:**
- "٢٠ دقيقة — بس" (then show the recipe from start to finish)
- "١ كيلو حمص في ٤ دقايق" (then show the blender)

**What does NOT work as a TikTok hook:**
- Presenter greeting ("أهلًا بيكم في صفحة هفار")
- Brand logo intro (first 2 seconds are brand logo over music)
- A slow camera pan around a kitchen
- Text-heavy opening slide
- Any spoken intro that delays showing the food or action

## Pacing and Editing Standards

### Cut on Action

Every cut should happen at the moment of action — the pour, the close of the lid, the moment food touches the surface. Cuts between static shots read as slow and amateurish. Cuts on action are satisfying and propel the viewer forward.

### Satisfying Transitions

For transformation content, the transition itself is a satisfaction moment. The standard cut is the strongest option. Avoid:
- Slow fades (kill the pace)
- Zoom-in/zoom-out transitions (overused, read as generic)
- Wipe transitions (dated)

Acceptable:
- Hard cut on action
- Match cut (the shape of an ingredient matches the shape of the appliance opening, cut at the pour moment)
- A quick reveal pull back from close-up detail to full scene

### Sound Matching

If using a trending sound, the visual cuts must match the audio rhythm — hit the beat with the action. If using original audio (the actual sound of the appliance operating, cooking sounds), this is often more effective than music for appliance-demo content. The real sound of a blender running, the real sound of an air fryer, the real sizzle — these are satisfying sounds that do not require music overlay.

### Text Overlays

Arabic text overlays on TikTok must be:
- Large — readable at a glance, without the viewer pausing the video
- High contrast — white on dark areas, or dark on light areas with sufficient contrast
- Brief — one line of text at a time, maximum 4–5 words
- Cairo typeface — consistent with brand identity (TikTok's built-in fonts are acceptable only if no brand font is available in the editing tool; prefer custom text overlays using the brand font)
- RTL direction — right-to-left text entry and alignment in all Arabic text overlays

**Text timing:** text overlays should appear for at least 1.5 seconds and no more than 3–4 seconds unless they are titles/credits. The viewer should be able to read the text without pausing.

### Duration Sweet Spots

| Content type | Optimal duration |
|-------------|-----------------|
| Recipe demo (full) | 45–60 seconds |
| Technique tip | 20–35 seconds |
| Before/after | 15–25 seconds |
| Kitchen organization | 30–45 seconds |
| Chef stitch/duet | 30–60 seconds (the chef's clip + Hvar addition) |

TikTok's algorithm does not inherently reward longer videos — it rewards high completion rates. A 20-second video with 85% completion beats a 60-second video with 40% completion every time.

## Brand Integration Rules

### The product must be naturally in use, not placed

If the Hvar blender is sitting on the counter but not being used in this video, remove it. Every Hvar product that appears in a TikTok video is there because it is doing something in this video. Product placement that doesn't serve the content is visible to the audience and reads as an advertisement.

### Product reveal: show it doing something impressive

The first time the product appears in a video should be at the moment it is doing something — not sitting idle, not being held up to the camera. The air fryer is opened to reveal a golden result. The blender is running with ingredients becoming smooth. The mixer is turning with dough forming.

**Wrong:** Camera pans across the counter to show the Hvar air fryer. "هنستخدم اليوم الفريير الهوائي من هفار —"

**Right:** Cut to the air fryer basket being opened. Smoke rises. The result is visible. The Hvar label is readable in frame at normal viewing distance.

### Label and logo visibility

The Hvar product label should be visible and readable in at least one clear shot in every video, but does not need to be the focus of any shot. Natural product placement — the label visible because the product is being used at a natural angle — is sufficient and more credible than an obvious label-forward shot.

A brief text overlay or end card naming the product is optional and acceptable. It should appear once, briefly, not as a recurring overlay throughout the video.

### End card standard

A simple 2-second end card showing:
- The product name in Arabic
- One trust line: "ضمان سنتين · شحن مجاني · ادفع عند الاستلام"
- Link to the product (in bio or as a TikTok link sticker)

No Hvar logo animation. No full brand identity treatment. Two seconds, functional, clear.

## What NOT to Do on TikTok

**Do not repurpose Facebook or Instagram content directly to TikTok.** The formats are different, the pacing is different, the expectation is different. A 4:5 Facebook product post does not work at 9:16 TikTok aspect. A 2-minute Facebook recipe video is not a TikTok video — it needs to be re-edited to TikTok rhythm.

**Do not use slow, beautiful photography as TikTok content.** The editorial photography direction in the brand visual system produces content that belongs on Instagram and the website. TikTok needs action, motion, and transformation. A beautiful still of food on a kitchen counter is an Instagram post. It is not a TikTok.

**Do not do talking-head brand announcements.** "مرحبًا بيكم في صفحة هفار، عندنا منتج جديد هنقدمه —" This format performs almost exclusively for established creators with loyal audiences. For a brand account, it is the most effective way to lose the audience in the first 3 seconds.

**Do not neglect trending sounds when they are relevant.** TikTok's algorithm has shown preference for content using currently trending audio. When a trending sound matches a content opportunity (a cooking sound trend, a recipe challenge), using it is the right call. Missing every trending moment for the sake of brand consistency at the audio level is a strategic error.

**Do not post at slow frequency.** TikTok rewards accounts that post consistently. The algorithm is more favorable to accounts posting 3–5 times per week than to accounts posting 2–3 times per month. If content production cannot support this frequency, the TikTok presence should be reconsidered — a sparse TikTok account is worse than no TikTok account.

**Do not use English captions or text overlays in content directed at the Egyptian audience.** The audience is Arabic-primary. English text overlays in content for Egyptian home cooks signal that the content was not made for them. Arabic only, Egyptian dialect where appropriate.
