# Tone of Voice — هفار

> How Hvar sounds. In Arabic and English. Per platform. Read this before writing any copy — product descriptions, social posts, interface copy, error messages, email subject lines, or anything else with words.

---

## Voice Pillars

Hvar's voice rests on three pillars. They apply in Arabic and English, across every platform and context. The energy level shifts per platform; the pillars do not.

### 1. Warm Authority

Hvar knows kitchen equipment better than anyone in the room, and it tells you about it the way a knowledgeable friend does — not a sales rep, not a textbook, not a condescending expert. The authority is real. The delivery is warm.

**What it sounds like in Arabic:**
- "الموتور ١٢٠٠ واط — ده هيخلّيكِ تعمل عجينة قديمة وعجينة كيك من غير ما الماكينة تسخن." *(1200W motor — that means you can do bread dough and cake batter without the machine overheating.)*
- NOT: "الطاقة الفائقة للمحرك تضمن أداءً متميزًا في جميع تطبيقات الطهي." *(generic MSA marketing-speak)*

**What it sounds like in English:**
- "1200W with a thermal cutout. Run it through bread dough for twenty minutes and it won't flinch."
- NOT: "Experience superior power performance across a wide range of culinary applications."

**What it is NOT:** Lecturing. Condescending. Overwhelming with specs. The authority earns trust — it does not demand submission to expertise.

---

### 2. Specific Kindness

Hvar is kind, but specifically kind — not generically warm. Generic warmth is indistinguishable from every other brand trying to be friendly. Specific kindness means: we know what you're cooking, we know when you're making it, we know the real problem you're trying to solve.

**What it sounds like in Arabic:**
- "العيد جاي — وهفار موجودة لما المطبخ يشتغل على آخره." *(Eid is coming — Hvar is there when the kitchen runs at full capacity.)*
- "قليل بس بيفرق — الهواء الساخن بيوصل لكل ناحية، والدجاج بيطلع مقرمش من كل ناحية." *(A small thing that matters — the hot air reaches every corner, the chicken comes out crispy all around.)*
- NOT: "نحن نهتم برحلتك في المطبخ!" *(We care about your kitchen journey! — generic marketing sentence)*

**What it sounds like in English:**
- "The basket seats a full-sized chicken. That matters during Eid."
- NOT: "Perfect for all your cooking needs!"

**What it is NOT:** Hollow. "We care!" without showing you what, specifically, we care about is not kindness — it is noise.

---

### 3. Confident Without Showing Off

Hvar does not say "the best." It does not say "unmatched." It does not need to claim a superlative because the specific detail it gives you does the work a superlative cannot. If the claim is real and the product delivers it, say the specific thing. If the claim is not provable, cut it.

**What it sounds like in Arabic:**
- "ضمان ٣ سنوات — من غير شروط مخبّية." *(3-year warranty — without hidden conditions.)*
- "ده مش بلاستيك عادي — الغطا بالستانلس الأصلي." *(This is not regular plastic — the lid is real stainless.)*
- NOT: "الأفضل في فئته!" *(The best in its class!)*
- NOT: "جودة لا مثيل لها!" *(Unmatched quality!)*

**What it sounds like in English:**
- "Three-year warranty, covered parts and labor."
- NOT: "Best-in-class quality you can trust."

**What it is NOT:** False modesty. Hvar knows it is better than Sokany. It does not say "best" — it shows you why without asking you to take its word for it.

## Arabic Tone

### The Register

Warm, educated Egyptian Arabic. Not formal Modern Standard Arabic (MSA). Not colloquial slang. The register is: a knowledgeable friend who speaks naturally, uses correct grammar, and never sounds like they're reading from a government document or texting on their phone.

**Phoneme of the register:** The way a chef or doctor friend talks when they're explaining something to you over coffee. Comfortable. Specific. Personal without being casual.

### Dialect Examples vs. Wrong Register

| Situation | Right (Egyptian Arabic, warm) | Wrong MSA | Wrong Slang |
|-----------|------------------------------|-----------|-------------|
| Product benefit | "هيخلّيكِ تطبخي أسرع وبجودة أحسن" | "يتيح لكِ الطهي بكفاءة عالية" | "بيعملك أكل زبالة بسرعة" |
| Trust statement | "الشحن مجاني وافحصي المنتج قبل ما تدفعي" | "يُقدَّم التوصيل دون رسوم مع إمكانية الفحص قبل السداد" | "ابعتيه لو مش عاجباكِ" |
| Warranty | "٣ سنين ضمان، مش كلام" | "نقدم ضمانًا قدره ثلاث سنوات" | "معاكِ ٣ سنين لو في مشكلة" |
| Empty state | "مفيش طلبات لحد دلوقتي — ابدئي أول طلبك" | "لا توجد طلبات مسبقة" | "معكِش طلبات" |
| Error message | "في مشكلة صغيرة — جربي تاني أو كلميني" | "حدث خطأ غير متوقع" | "في بعبع" |

### Gender Agreement

The primary audience is female. All second-person address defaults to feminine: `اختاري`, `أضيفي`, `سجّلي`, `جربي`. When context is clearly mixed or male, adjust. Default is feminine.

### Definite Articles and Hamza

Use correct Arabic. `أضيفي للسلة` not `اضيفي للسلة`. The hamza matters. Bad Arabic typography is immediately visible to native speakers and signals low quality. It is a brand perception failure.

## English Tone

### The Register

Clean. Warm. Confident. No corporate jargon. No fake cheerfulness. No "amazing!" energy. The English voice is calibrated for partners, international contexts, technical documentation, and the secondary English-comfortable audience.

Think: an editor who has been to Egypt, respects the brand, and knows how to write.

**In practice:**
- Short sentences. One idea per sentence.
- Active voice. "The basket fits a full chicken." Not "A full chicken can be accommodated by the basket."
- No exclamation points in product descriptions. Exclamation points are fine in social captions where energy is appropriate — never in product UI copy or emails.
- Specificity over superlative. Always.

### What Hvar English Sounds Like vs. What It Does Not

| Context | Right | Wrong |
|---------|-------|-------|
| Product tagline | "Built for real Egyptian kitchens." | "Amazing quality for all your cooking needs!" |
| Warranty | "3-year warranty, parts and labor." | "We've got you covered with our comprehensive warranty program!" |
| CTA | "Add to cart" | "Get yours now!" |
| Empty cart | "Your cart is empty. Browse products." | "Oops! Looks like your cart is feeling a little lonely!" |
| Shipping | "Free delivery across Egypt." | "Enjoy free shipping on all orders!" |
| COD | "Pay when your order arrives. Inspect first." | "Shop with confidence knowing you can pay later!" |

## Platform Tone Variations

The same three voice pillars. Different energy per platform. The voice is consistent; the volume and register vary.

### hvarstore.com (Considered, Warm)

The website is the most deliberate context. Copy here has the most time to be read. Use it.

- Product names: Cairo Black in Arabic, product category + model. No marketing suffixes ("Pro", "Ultra", "Max") unless that is the actual product name.
- Product descriptions: specific, warm, benefit-led. Lead with what the product enables ("ده هيخليكِ تعمل..."), follow with the specification that proves it.
- Trust copy: plain and confident. "افحصي المنتج قبل ما تدفعي" — not "enjoy the peace of mind of our inspect-before-you-pay policy."
- UI microcopy: functional and warm, never cold. "أضيفي للسلة" not "ADD TO CART". "طلباتي" not "ORDER HISTORY".
- Error messages: specific enough to help, warm enough not to alarm. "في مشكلة في الاتصال — جربي تاني." Never a raw error code.

### Instagram Caption (Conversational, Inviting)

Instagram is for looking first. The caption adds context and invites engagement. It is shorter than Facebook.

- Lead with the food or the action, not the product: "الكنافة طلعت مظبوطة في أول مرة." Then context. Then soft product mention. Then CTA.
- Ask one question or invite one action. Not multiple.
- Energy is warm and curious, not promotional.
- No hashtag forest in the caption body — put hashtags in a comment or at the end after a line break.
- Egyptian Arabic always. Product names in Arabic when possible.

### Facebook Post (Direct, Community-Flavored)

Facebook is Hvar's community home. The audience here is wider, the attention span is shorter, and the native behavior is "share this to the group."

- The first sentence is the hook. If it doesn't earn a scroll, the rest doesn't matter.
- Videos get captions in Arabic — always. Not everyone watches with sound.
- Community language: ask for shares, ask for opinions, respond to comments with the same warm authority voice.
- Product posts can be more direct about the offer (price, installments, COD) than Instagram.
- Recipe posts don't lead with the product — they lead with the dish. "مكرونة بشاميل بالفرن الهوائي — أسرع وأقل دهون." Then the product.

### TikTok Hook (Punchy, Curious)

TikTok gets two seconds. Use them.

- The hook is always a question or a provocative statement: "هتعمل إيه لو كنتِ قادرة تشوي فراخ بدون فرن؟"
- No preamble. Start in the middle of the action.
- Captions are short — they supplement what's on screen, they don't repeat it.
- The product name appears in the video, not forced into the caption.
- The tone is the most casual of all platforms — still warm authority, but at TikTok pace.

### WhatsApp Message Template (Practical, Human)

WhatsApp communications (order confirmation, shipping update, service ticket update) are practical first. The audience is in their messaging app — they want the information, not a brand experience.

- Short. The most important information in the first sentence.
- No marketing copy in transactional messages. If someone's order is confirmed, confirm it. The upsell is not here.
- Warm but efficient: "طلبك رقم HVAR-XXXX تم تأكيده وبيتجهز للشحن. هنبعتلك التتبع قريبًا."
- NOT: "أهلًا وسهلًا بيكِ في هفار! يسعدنا إخبارك أن طلبك الكريم قد تم استلامه وسيتم معالجته في أقرب وقت."

### Email (Clear, Friendly, No Fluff)

Email is the most formal channel in the mix, but it should not read like a corporate newsletter.

- Subject lines: specific and direct. "طلبك تم التأكيد — رقم HVAR-XXXX" not "تحديث مهم بخصوص طلبك"
- Body: short paragraphs, one idea each. If there's a CTA, one CTA — not three.
- Never: email newsletter jargon, unnecessary headers and sub-headers for a 3-sentence update
- The footer is functional — contact info, WhatsApp, unsubscribe. Not a brand manifesto.

## What Hvar Never Says

These are disqualifying phrases. If any of these appear in any piece of Hvar copy, cut them.

**Arabic never-say list:**

- **"الأفضل في فئته"** (best in its class) — unverifiable superlative. Not our word.
- **"جودة لا مثيل لها"** (unmatched quality) — empty claim. Show the quality, don't claim it.
- **"سارع بالحجز"** (hurry and order) — urgency language. Destroys the premium feeling instantly.
- **"عرض لفترة محدودة"** (limited time offer) — unless it is factually and specifically limited, with a real end date shown. Never as a rhetorical device.
- **"رائع"**, **"مذهل"**, **"خرافي"** as brand copy — these words read as empty hype. Use them if a chef or customer uses them. Never in brand voice.
- **"كليك هنا"** (click here) — use meaningful link copy: "شوفي المنتج", "اطلبيه دلوقتي", "تعرفي على الضمان"
- Broken Arabic: any copy with hamza errors, tashkeel omitted where it is needed for clarity, or mixed Arabic/Franco-Arabic within a single brand message
- Transliteration of English when Arabic equivalent exists: "أوردر" when "طلب" works, "برودكت" when "منتج" is the right word

**English never-say list:**

- "Amazing" / "incredible" / "fantastic" — these words have been used so often they mean nothing
- "Journey" when describing a purchase or usage experience — corporate buzzword
- "Seamless" — overused to the point of meaningless, especially in tech/ecommerce contexts
- "Game-changer" — the game is not changing. A blender is a blender. An excellent blender.
- Any question in a subject line: "Are you ready to upgrade your kitchen?" — rhetorical and manipulative
- "Don't miss out" / "Last chance" unless the urgency is factually true and specifically stated

## Numbers and Pricing

**Egyptian numerals vs. Western numerals:** Use Eastern Arabic numerals (١٢٣٤٥) in all Arabic-context UI: product prices, quantities, warranty years, order numbers shown in Arabic context. Use Western Arabic numerals (12345) in English-context UI, technical codes, and when Eastern numerals would break layout alignment. The rule is consistency within a context, not mixing within a single element.

**Currency:**
- Arabic UI: `١٢٩٩ جنيه` — the amount (Eastern numerals in Inter tabular), then `جنيه` (Cairo). No space between number and currency is also acceptable: `١٢٩٩جنيه`. Pick one and be consistent.
- English UI or dual-language context: `EGP 1,299` — Western format, comma separator, no decimal for round numbers.
- Never: `LE` (an unofficial abbreviation), `L.E.` (older), mixed formats in the same UI.

**Installment language:**
- Arabic: "٨٣ جنيه شهريًا على ١٢ شهر" — specific, not "قسّطيها بكل سهولة"
- Show the provider name: "عن طريق فاليو / سهولة / أمان"
- Show both: the monthly amount and the total number of months. Do not show just one.
- Never show an installment amount without the term and provider clearly adjacent. An installment amount without context is misleading.
